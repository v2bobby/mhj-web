THE REALITY IN LAGOS

A food system
under pressure.

Lagos faces interconnected crises that linear agriculture cannot solve. Mayhaj Farm was designed from first principles to address all three simultaneously.

Protein Deficit

Lagos consumes 11 million eggs daily, yet local supply suffers from a structural 40% deficit. The result: price volatility, inconsistent quality, and long supply chains that compromise freshness.

Soil Degradation

Over-reliance on synthetic NPK has stripped urban soils of microbial life and structure. Water retention declines. Gardening costs rise. Long-term food resilience erodes.

Waste as Liability

Traditional poultry operations generate nitrogen-rich waste that, when improperly managed, contaminates waterways and creates neighbor disputes. This is lost economic value and environmental harm.
