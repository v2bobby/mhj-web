### The Opportunity: Lagos Agri-Food System Under Pressure

Lagos presents a unique convergence of urgent needs and latent demand.

**1. Protein Deficit & Supply Fragility**  
Lagos consumes approximately 11 million eggs daily. Local production capacity lags significantly, creating a structural ~40% deficit. This gap manifests as price volatility, inconsistent quality, and heavy reliance on long-haul supply chains that compromise freshness.

**2. Soil Degradation Crisis**  
Decades of over-reliance on synthetic NPK fertilizers have stripped urban and peri-urban soils of microbial life, organic matter, and structure. The result: declining water retention, higher input costs for gardeners and smallholders, and long-term threats to local food resilience.

**3. Waste Management Failure**  
Traditional poultry operations in areas like Ikorodu generate substantial volumes of nitrogen-rich waste. When mismanaged, this creates environmental hazards (waterway contamination, odor complaints) and destroys potential economic value. Current disposal practices represent both an ecological liability and a missed opportunity.

These are not isolated problems. They are interconnected symptoms of a linear agricultural model that Mayhaj Farm is designed to disrupt.

### The Mayhaj Solution: Closed-Loop Systems Design

Mayhaj Farm replaces the traditional linear flow (feed → eggs → waste) with a circular, value-multiplying architecture.

**Core Model**
- **200 Isa Brown layers** housed in modified, biosecure container systems engineered for Lagos' climate realities.
- Daily waste (droppings) is captured and fed into a purpose-built 3-bay composting facility.
- Through a precise 28-day thermophilic aerobic process, waste is transformed into a premium, sieved, moisture-controlled organic fertilizer.
- Revenue is generated from two high-demand streams: Grade-A brown eggs and regenerative fertilizer.

**Why This Works**
- **Risk Hedging:** Fertilizer revenue provides a buffer against feed cost spikes and egg price fluctuations.
- **Resource Efficiency:** Waste becomes the primary input for the second product line — dramatically lowering net operating costs.
- **Market Alignment:** Eggs serve immediate protein demand; fertilizer serves the rapidly expanding urban organic gardening, rooftop farming, and premium landscaping sectors in Lekki, Magodo, Ikeja, and beyond.
- **Environmental Alpha:** Pathogen neutralization at 60°C+, odor elimination, and measurable soil health improvements (target: +25% water retention in treated soils).

This is precision agriculture meets circular economy thinking — executed at a scale and location that maximizes both impact and commercial viability.

### Technical Production Protocols

**4.1 High-Efficiency Layer Management**  
We deploy a modified container-based housing system that delivers superior biosecurity compared to traditional open-air pens. The controlled environment maintains consistent 85–90% lay rates even during Lagos' extreme weather swings. Strict protocols around foot-dips, visitor quarantine, and daily management minimize disease risk.

**4.2 The Composting Bio-Chemistry (28-Day Process)**  
Our fertilizer is not dried manure. It is a biologically active soil amendment engineered through science:

- **Phase 1 — Heating (Days 1–7):** High-nitrogen droppings balanced with carbon-rich sawdust. Temperatures reach 60°C, neutralizing pathogens and weed seeds while initiating decomposition.
- **Phase 2 — Curing (Days 8–21):** Material is turned every 7 days to maintain oxygenation. Microbial communities mature and stabilize.
- **Phase 3 — Refining (Days 22–28):** Final product is sieved to fine, uniform texture and moisture-controlled for optimal handling and application by urban users.

The result: An odor-free, easy-to-apply, premium-grade organic fertilizer positioned for high-end garden centers and estate landscaping contracts.
